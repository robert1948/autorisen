# CapeControl LinkedIn Video Production and Rollout Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Owner:** Founder-led GTM (Robert)
**Channel:** LinkedIn (primary), with repurposing to website and onboarding flows
**Positioning Anchor:** Advanced governed AI agents for high-trust, compliance-heavy operations

## 1. Strategic Goal

Use short, trust-first instructional video content to convert skeptical compliance-heavy operators from passive awareness into active evaluation of CapeControl's advanced AI agent offering.

Business outcomes to drive:
- Increase qualified inbound conversations from regulated SMBs
- Improve onboarding starts from LinkedIn traffic
- Reduce "black-box AI" objections in sales/discovery calls
- Establish CapeControl as advanced, evidence-first, and governance-led
- Increase advanced-agent demo requests from operations and risk stakeholders

## 2. Messaging Guardrails (Must Stay True)

All videos should reinforce the same core statements:
- Evidence is the currency
- Approved sources only
- Human checkpoints in every critical workflow
- Advanced agents run real operational sequences, not isolated prompts
- Agent actions are reviewable, traceable, and role-governed
- Calm before complexity
- Not another chatbot

Avoid in video copy:
- "Fully autonomous" claims
- "Replace your team" language
- Absolute certainty promises (for example, "perfect trust")
- Generic hype phrasing without concrete controls
- Capability claims without showing the execution trail

## 3. Audience and Content Tracks

Primary audience:
- Compliance-heavy operators in SMBs (finance, legal, property, maintenance, operations)

Secondary audience:
- Technical evaluators and transformation leads
- Founder/operator peers who influence buying

Video tracks:
1. Trust and governance explainers
1. Advanced agent workflow walkthroughs (how work is done end-to-end)
1. Evidence-pack proof clips
1. Agent capability spotlights (planner, retriever, reviewer, escalator)
1. Founder POV clips (problem framing + positioning)

## 4. Content Architecture

## 4.1 Hero series (first 6 weeks)

Launch with 6 core videos:
1. The Problem: Why "black-box AI" fails compliance teams (60-90s)
1. Offering Overview: Advanced agents + governed execution model (90-120s)
1. Multi-Step Demo: Input source to audit-ready result (120-180s)
1. Proof Clip: "Where did this come from?" with source trace (45-75s)
1. Human-in-Control Clip: Checkpoints and escalation in practice (60-90s)
1. Founder Narrative: From automation risk to governed agent operations (60-90s)

## 4.2 Post format mix

Weekly mix:
- 1 anchor video post (native LinkedIn)
- 1 short proof clip
- 1 capability spotlight micro-clip
- 1 text post with still frame or carousel pullout from video

Format specs:
- Aspect: 4:5 for feed priority, optional 1:1 variant
- Duration target: 45-120s (demos may run longer)
- Captions: mandatory burned-in + SRT
- Hook window: first 2-3 seconds must state the pain or question

## 5. Production System

## 5.1 Pre-production (weekly)

1. Pick one objection/theme from live conversations
1. Define one video objective and one CTA
1. Draft script with this structure:
- Hook: operational pain
- Contrast: generic AI output vs governed advanced-agent execution
- Proof: show UI, source trail, and checkpoint artifacts
- Capability moment: show one concrete agent behavior
- CTA: one next action
1. Build shot list and on-screen text overlays
1. Compliance/accuracy check (claims and terminology)

## 5.2 Production workflow

1. Record UI walkthrough in staging-safe environment
1. Record founder voiceover (or founder-to-camera intro)
1. Edit for pace (remove dead time, emphasize proof moments)
1. Add captions and highlight overlays:
- Source citation
- Agent action log
- Approval checkpoint
- Audit trail event
1. Export platform variants:
- LinkedIn feed master
- Website/onboarding embed version

## 5.3 Quality control checklist

- Does the video show real product behavior?
- Does the video demonstrate a concrete advanced agent capability?
- Are trust controls visible on-screen?
- Is every claim supportable by product behavior?
- Is audio clear and captioned?
- Is CTA singular and measurable?

## 8. Measurement Framework

## 8.1 Channel KPIs (LinkedIn)

- 3-second and 50% view rates
- Completion rate
- Saves, shares, and quality comments
- Profile clicks and website clicks from video posts
- Demo intent signal rate (comments/DMs requesting a walkthrough)

## 8.2 Funnel KPIs (business impact)

- Onboarding starts from LinkedIn UTM traffic
- Discovery calls/messages from target roles
- Advanced-agent demo requests from target roles
- Time-to-first-workflow from LinkedIn-acquired users
- Trust checkpoint completion in onboarding

## 8.3 Success thresholds (first 6 weeks)

- Completion rate > 25% on sub-90s videos
- Meaningful comment rate > baseline by 2x
- LinkedIn-sourced onboarding starts growing week-over-week
- At least 1 advanced-agent demo request trendline from week 2 onward
- At least 2 repeating objection categories resolved by new clips

## 9. Experiment Backlog

Run one experiment per week:
1. Hook framing: pain question vs bold statement
1. CTA framing: "View a traceable workflow demo" vs "See how governed AI work runs"
1. Founder-on-camera intro vs voiceover-only
1. 45-60s cut vs 90-120s cut
1. Capability-first opening vs risk-first opening
1. English-only vs bilingual (EN + Afrikaans subtitles)

## 10. Team Roles and Operating Rhythm

Minimum operating roles:
- Founder/Host: narrative, voice, authority
- Producer/Editor: editing, captions, variant exports
- Reviewer (product/compliance): factual and governance accuracy

Weekly rhythm:
- Monday: topic + script draft
- Tuesday: recording
- Wednesday: edit + QA
- Thursday: publish anchor clip
- Friday: insight review and next script brief

## 11. Risk Management

Key risks:
- Over-claiming autonomy
- Drifting into generic AI language
- Publishing product behavior that changes quickly
- Positioning advanced capability without showing sufficient proof

Mitigations:
- Mandatory pre-publish claims check
- Keep scripts tied to current UI build/version
- Use dated captions for rapidly evolving features when needed
- Require one visible evidence trail element in every capability clip

### 13.2 Weekly production backlog template

For each weekly video unit, track:
- Objective: one primary business outcome (awareness, proof, conversion)
- Persona: one target role (operator, risk lead, compliance reviewer, technical evaluator)
- Capability focus: planner, retriever, reviewer, escalator, or end-to-end workflow
- Proof artifact: source citation, action log, checkpoint event, or audit-ready output
- CTA: one approved CTA from Appendix B
- KPI target: completion rate, comment quality, demo intent signal, or onboarding start

### 13.3 Dependencies and prerequisites

- Product dependency: stable staging workflow that demonstrates the selected capability
- Data dependency: approved examples/screens and sanitized sample records
- Compliance dependency: reviewer sign-off for claims, wording, and visual overlays
- Analytics dependency: UTM naming standard and weekly scorecard update cadence
- Distribution dependency: founder publishing windows and comment-response coverage in first 60 minutes

### 13.4 Weekly operating checklist (definition of done)

1. Script approved against messaging guardrails in section 2.
1. Video includes at least one explicit proof artifact on-screen.
1. Advanced capability is demonstrated with real product behavior.
1. Human checkpoint or role-governed control is visible.
1. Captions are complete and accurate.
1. CTA is singular and mapped to one measurable KPI.
1. UTM link is attached and validated before publish.
1. Post-publish metrics and objections are logged within 24 hours.

### 13.5 Risks to timeline and contingency actions

- Risk: feature/UI drift invalidates recorded demo.
  Contingency: maintain a short evergreen proof-clip library and swap to stable capability clips.
- Risk: low comment quality despite views.
  Contingency: test problem-first hooks and add role-specific captions in next two posts.
- Risk: capability claims interpreted as autonomy claims.
  Contingency: add explicit human-checkpoint callouts and reviewer overlays in edit phase.
- Risk: production bottleneck (editing turnaround).
  Contingency: pre-build lower thirds, intro/outro, and overlay templates for rapid reuse.
