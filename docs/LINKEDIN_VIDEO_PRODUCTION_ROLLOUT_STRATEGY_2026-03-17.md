# CapeControl LinkedIn Video Production and Rollout Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Owner:** Founder-led GTM (Robert)
**Channel:** LinkedIn (primary), with repurposing to website and onboarding flows
**Positioning Anchor:** Governed AI agents for high-trust, compliance-heavy operations

## 1. Strategic Goal
Use short, trust-first instructional video content to convert skeptical compliance-heavy operators from passive awareness into active onboarding.

Business outcomes to drive:
- Increase qualified inbound conversations from regulated SMBs
- Improve onboarding starts from LinkedIn traffic
- Reduce "black-box AI" objections in sales/discovery calls
- Establish CapeControl as evidence-first and governance-led

## 2. Messaging Guardrails (Must Stay True)
All videos should reinforce the same core statements:
- Evidence is the currency
- Approved sources only
- Human checkpoints in every critical workflow
- Calm before complexity
- Not another chatbot

Avoid in video copy:
- "Fully autonomous" claims
- "Replace your team" language
- Absolute certainty promises (for example, "perfect trust")
- Generic hype phrasing without concrete controls

## 3. Audience and Content Tracks
Primary audience:
- Compliance-heavy operators in SMBs (finance, legal, property, maintenance, operations)

Secondary audience:
- Technical evaluators and transformation leads
- Founder/operator peers who influence buying

Video tracks:
1. Trust and governance explainers
2. Workflow walkthroughs (how work is done end-to-end)
3. Evidence-pack proof clips
4. Founder POV clips (problem framing + positioning)

## 4. Content Architecture
## 4.1 Hero series (first 6 weeks)
Launch with 6 core videos:
1. The Problem: Why "black-box AI" fails audits (60-90s)
2. How CapeControl Works: Agents, evidence, checkpoints (90-120s)
3. First Workflow Demo: From task to approved output (120-180s)
4. Proof Clip: Where did this number come from? (45-75s)
5. Governance Clip: Approval and escalation path in practice (60-90s)
6. Founder Narrative: Human middleware to governed execution (60-90s)

## 4.2 Post format mix
Weekly mix:
- 1 anchor video post (native LinkedIn)
- 1 short proof clip
- 1 text post with still frame or carousel pullout from video

Format specs:
- Aspect: 4:5 for feed priority, optional 1:1 variant
- Duration target: 45-120s (demos may run longer)
- Captions: mandatory burned-in + SRT
- Hook window: first 2-3 seconds must state the pain or question

## 5. Production System
## 5.1 Pre-production (weekly)
1. Pick one objection/theme from live conversations
2. Define one video objective and one CTA
3. Draft script with this structure:
- Hook: operational pain
- Contrast: black box vs governed flow
- Proof: show UI and source/approval artifacts
- CTA: one next action
4. Build shot list and on-screen text overlays
5. Compliance/accuracy check (claims and terminology)

## 5.2 Production workflow
1. Record UI walkthrough in staging-safe environment
2. Record founder voiceover (or founder-to-camera intro)
3. Edit for pace (remove dead time, emphasize proof moments)
4. Add captions and highlight overlays:
- Source citation
- Approval checkpoint
- Audit trail event
5. Export platform variants:
- LinkedIn feed master
- Website/onboarding embed version

## 5.3 Quality control checklist
- Does the video show real product behavior?
- Are trust controls visible on-screen?
- Is every claim supportable by product behavior?
- Is audio clear and captioned?
- Is CTA singular and measurable?

## 6. Rollout Plan (6 Weeks)
## Week 0: Setup
- Define KPI baseline from current LinkedIn and site analytics
- Create template package (intro slate, lower third, CTA card)
- Prepare tracking links (UTM per post/video)

## Week 1: Positioning launch
- Post V1: "Most AI tools are black boxes" (problem framing)
- Post V2: "How CapeControl is different" (governance summary)
- Goal: establish narrative and collect objection language

## Week 2: Proof-first
- Post V3: First workflow walkthrough
- Post V4: 45s evidence citation clip
- Goal: prove traceability claims with concrete UI evidence

## Week 3: Oversight and control
- Post V5: Human checkpoint and escalation walkthrough
- Post V6: "Tools off by default" setup clip
- Goal: reduce risk anxiety from decision-makers

## Week 4: Vertical resonance
- Create 2 sector-tailored edits (for example legal + property/ops)
- Publish with role-specific hooks and examples
- Goal: improve relevance and qualified inbound quality

## Week 5: Social proof and objections
- Compile common comments/DM objections into FAQ clip
- Post before/after workflow story (anonymized)
- Goal: increase comment quality and conversion intent

## Week 6: Conversion sprint
- Best-performing format rerun with stronger CTA
- Publish onboarding invitation clip for closed beta/testing cohort
- Goal: convert engaged viewers into onboarding starts/calls

Secondary amplification:

- DM follow-up flow for high-intent commenters

Repurposing:
- Embed top 3 videos on `/how-it-works`
- Add short clips in onboarding trust screens
- Reuse transcript segments in text posts and email outreach

## 8. Measurement Framework
## 8.1 Channel KPIs (LinkedIn)
- 3-second and 50% view rates
- Completion rate
- Saves, shares, and quality comments
- Profile clicks and website clicks from video posts

## 8.2 Funnel KPIs (business impact)
- Onboarding starts from LinkedIn UTM traffic
- Discovery calls/messages from target roles
- Time-to-first-workflow from LinkedIn-acquired users
- Trust checkpoint completion in onboarding

## 8.3 Success thresholds (first 6 weeks)
- Completion rate > 25% on sub-90s videos
- Meaningful comment rate > baseline by 2x
- LinkedIn-sourced onboarding starts growing week-over-week
- At least 2 repeating objection categories resolved by new clips

## 9. Experiment Backlog
Run one experiment per week:
1. Hook framing: pain question vs bold statement
2. CTA framing: "See How It Works" vs "Start Guided Onboarding"
3. Founder-on-camera intro vs voiceover-only
4. 45-60s cut vs 90-120s cut
5. English-only vs bilingual (EN + Afrikaans subtitles)

## 10. Team Roles and Operating Rhythm
Minimum operating roles:
- Founder/Host: narrative, voice, authority
- Producer/Editor: editing, captions, variant exports
- Reviewer (product/compliance): factual and governance accuracy

Weekly rhythm:
- Monday: topic + script draft
- Tuesday: recording
- Wednesday: edit + QA
- Thursday: publish anchor clip
- Friday: insight review and next script brief

## 11. Risk Management
Key risks:
- Over-claiming autonomy
- Drifting into generic AI language
- Publishing product behavior that changes quickly

Mitigations:
- Mandatory pre-publish claims check
- Keep scripts tied to current UI build/version
- Use dated captions for rapidly evolving features when needed
